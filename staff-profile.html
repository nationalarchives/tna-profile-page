Kostas Ntanos is the Head of Conservation Research & Development in the Collection Care department. After studying conservation of archaeological finds in Greece, in 2005 he completed a master’s degree in Conservation Science at the Royal College of Art and Victoria and Albert Museum joint conservation course. His current research interests include the assessment and management of environmental conditions in storage, the use of the technical history of archival records to aid interpretation of documentary evidence, and the use of technology in conservation practice and decision-making.
